Tips and Tricks to avoid osteoprosis
by dr. Hendra Hermanto, SpOT, M.Kes

Exercise Routine

To prevent osteoporosis, exercise regularly. Active movement and exercise can increase bone density and strength, especially if done regularly from childhood and adolescence. Schedule a favorite sport to do every day, such as weight and resistance training to strengthen muscles and bones. Regular exercise can help build up bone health for the future, especially since bone density reaches its maximum peak at around 30 years old.

Adequate Calcium Needs

Calcium is essential for strong bones, so it's important to consume enough calcium daily. You can meet your daily calcium needs by eating a variety of low-fat dairy products, high-calcium cereals, soy products, almonds, and green vegetables.

Take Vitamin D

Taking Vitamin D is important for maintaining healthy bones. It helps the body absorb calcium and maintain phosphorus levels in the blood. You can get Vitamin D by spending 10-15 minutes in the sun regularly, while still using sunscreen. You can consume vitamin D-rich foods and drinks, such as eggs, milk, cereals, salmon, tuna, and others. If necessary, take vitamin D supplements as recommended by your doctor.

Avoid Bad Habits

Implementing a healthy lifestyle involves avoiding smoking and alcohol consumption, as well as reducing the intake of soft drinks. Building healthy and strong bones begins with these simple steps. This is because alcohol can reduce the body's ability to store calcium, while soft drinks can trigger the release of calcium, leading to a reduction in bone density.

Get Screened Periodically

You can take a bone mineral density test regularly to evaluate the possibility of osteoporosis. This test will measure the amount of calcium and other minerals in your bones, especially your spine, pelvic bones, and bones in your wrist using X-ray technology.

Maintain a Healthy Weight

Maintaining a healthy weight can help reduce the risk of osteoporosis. Being underweight or obese can increase the risk of bone deterioration.
