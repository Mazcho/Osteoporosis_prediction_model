3 Risks of Osteoporosis

Osteoporosis can seriously affect daily activities. It increases the risk of fractures, reduces quality of life, and limits mobility. It can also cause significant psychological stress. Proper management of osteoporosis is crucial for maintaining optimal health and quality of life.

High risk of bone fracture

Osteoporosis causes bones to become brittle and prone to breaking, particularly in the hips, spine, and wrists. These fractures can result in severe pain and mobility issues.

Decreased Quality of Life

Osteoporosis can interfere with daily activities and reduce quality of life due to the high risk of fracture. Pain and limited mobility can impair independence and psychological well-being.

Health Complications

Osteoporosis not only increases the risk of bone fractures but also of developing additional health problems, such as urinary tract infections and pneumonia. These health issues can worsen the overall health condition.
